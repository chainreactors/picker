Since the new [UN cybercrime treaty](https://www.eff.org/issues/un-cybercrime-treaty) began to take shape in 2022, EFF has been fighting on behalf of users to make sure content-based crimes are excluded from the Treaty, and robust human rights safeguards and rule of law standards are the basis of any final product.

There’s a lot at stake—the proposed [UN cybercrime treaty](https://www.eff.org/issues/un-cybercrime-treaty) has the potential to rewrite criminal laws around the world, adding new offenses and creating new police powers for both domestic and international investigations, and implicating the rights of billions of people worldwide.

Our push for human rights safeguards in the UN treaty follows a [campaign since 2013](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2013/07/thirteen-principles-for-human-rights) to strengthen human rights protections in government investigative powers.
