It’s been [a good year](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/12/right-repair-legislation-and-advocacy-2022-year-review) for right to repair advocates. [Colorado passed](https://www.usnews.com/news/health-news/articles/2022-05-31/despite-a-first-ever-right-to-repair-law-theres-no-easy-fix-for-wheelchair-users) an important law to allow wheelchair users access to resources they need to fix their own chairs. The Federal Trade Commission has stepped up enforcement of companies that limit the right to repair. And [New York made history](https://www.governor.ny.gov/news/governor-hochul-signs-digital-fair-repair-act-law) by passing the first broad consumer right to repair legislation at the end of 2022, requiring some digital electronics manufacturers to provide access to parts, tools, and information necessary for repairing their products.

Thank you to everyone who wrote in to support these bills, and especially to our allies in the Repair Coalition who lead this fight. Despite these wins, however, it’s important that those who care about the right to repair keep pushing to build on these steps.
