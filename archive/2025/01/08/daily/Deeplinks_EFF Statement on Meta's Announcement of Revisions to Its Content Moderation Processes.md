*Update: After this blog post was published (addressing [Meta's blog post here](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/01/meta-more-speech-fewer-mistakes)), we learned Meta also [revised](https://www.independent.co.uk/tech/facebook-fact-checking-hate-speech-meta-b2675311.html) its public "[Hateful Conduct](https://transparency.meta.com/en-gb/policies/community-standards/hateful-conduct/#policy-details)" policy in ways EFF finds concerning. We address these changes in [this blog post](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/01/metas-new-content-policy-will-harm-vulnerable-users-if-it-really-valued-free), published January 9, 2025.*

In general, EFF supports moves that bring more freedom of expression and transparency to platforms—regardless of their political motivation. We’re encouraged by [Meta's recognition](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/01/meta-more-speech-fewer-mistakes) that automated flagging and responses to flagged content have caused all sorts of mistakes in moderation.
