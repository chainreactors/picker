# A Guide to Indicators of Compromise (IoC) Analysis

## Indicators of Compromise (IoCs) play a pivotal role in this process by serving as breadcrumbs left behind by malicious actors. This article explores the significance of IoC analysis, the various types of IoCs, and effective strategies for leveraging them in the pursuit of cybersecurity.

> Indicators of Compromise are artifacts or traces that suggest a system has been compromised or that an ongoing security incident is taking place. These indicators can manifest in various forms, providing valuable insights into the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) employed by attackers.

### Types of IoCs:

**— File-Based IoCs:** Hash values, file names, and digital signatures associated with malicious files.
 **— Network-Based IoCs:** Suspicious IP addresses, domains, or URLs involved in malicious activities.
 **— Host-Based IoCs:** Unusual behavior, registry modifications, or unauthorized access on a compromised system.
 **— Behavioral IoCs:** Anomalies in system or network behavior that may indicate a security incident.
