Texas State Representative Steve Toth recently introduced a bill that targets the most viable form of safe and effective abortion access today—[medication abortion](https://twitter.com/HeyJaneHealth/status/1631429259976638464).

[House Bill (HB) 2690](https://capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/88R/billtext/pdf/HB02690I.pdf#navpanes=0) seeks to prevent the sale and distribution of abortion pills like Mifepristone and misoprostol, but it doesn’t stop there. By restricting access to certain information online, the bill seeks to prevent people from learning about abortion drugs, or even being aware of their existence. It would also systematically incentivize people and companies to silence anyone who wants to speak about abortion pills.

If passed, HB2690 would make it illegal to “provide information on how to obtain an abortion-inducing drug.” This includes stopping people from making or maintaining websites or creating and distributing applications on the topic.
