## 'Perils of the job' we're told

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Jessica Lyons](/Author/Jessica-Lyons "Read more by this author")

Mon 6 Feb 2023  //
23:59 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

A top US cyber diplomat said his Twitter account was compromised over the weekend.

Nate Fick, the inaugural US ambassador at large for Cyberspace and Digital Policy, on Saturday announced the hack of his personal account (not the government agency one) with - of course - a [tweet](https://twitter.com/ncfick/status/1622023054124617729).

"Perils of the job," he added, suggesting that his sense of irony remains intact. Assuming that was an authorized tweet, of course.

> My account has been hacked. Perils of the job…
>
> — Nate Fick (@ncfick) [February 5, 2023](https://twitter.com/ncfick/status/1622023054124617729?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw)

The US Department of State did not immediately respond to *The Register*'s questions about who was responsible for the attack, how they accessed Fick's account, or whether the miscreants posted any nonsense, such as the [cryptocurrency endorsements](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2022/dec/26/hack-of-twitter-uk-education-secretary-gillian-keegan-changes-profile-picture-elon-musk) that appeared on the hacked account of UK education secretary Gillian Keegan after her Twitter account was compromised.
