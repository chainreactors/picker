# EQT buys majority share in Swiss cybersecurity biz Acronis

![comment bubble on white](/design_picker/f5daacc84b9722c1e31ba85f836c37e4ad993fc4/graphics/icons/bubble_comment_white.png)](https://forums.theregister.com/forum/all/2024/08/07/eqt_buys_shares_acronis/ "View comments on this article")

## Went at equivalent of $3.5B+ valuation for entire firm, though portion sold not specified

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Laura Dobberstein](/Author/Laura-Dobberstein "Read more by this author")

Wed 7 Aug 2024  //
10:06 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

Acronis, the Swiss disaster recovery turned cybersecurity firm and catch-all for managed service providers, has been majority acquired by Europe’s largest private equity firm, EQT.

"We are thrilled to have EQT as a major shareholder to support our strategic expansion and share our vision for growth," [enthused](https://eqtgroup.com/news/2024/eqt-to-acquire-a-majority-stake-in-acronis-a-leading-cybersecurity-and-data-protection-platform-for-managed-service-providers-and-corporate-it-departments/) Acronis's CEO, Ezequiel Steiner.

"We would like to thank our existing investors for their support to date and are pleased that many will remain invested as we move forward," he added.
