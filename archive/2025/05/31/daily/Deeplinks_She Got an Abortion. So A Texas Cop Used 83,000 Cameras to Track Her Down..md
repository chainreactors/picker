![Automated License Plate Readers](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/sls-alpr-2023.png?itok=IJhFmYP5 "Automated License Plate Readers")

In a chilling sign of how far law enforcement surveillance has encroached on personal liberties, [*404 Media* recently revealed](https://www.404media.co/a-texas-cop-searched-license-plate-cameras-nationwide-for-a-woman-who-got-an-abortion/) that a sheriff’s office in Texas searched data from more than 83,000 [automated license plate reader (ALPR)](https://sls.eff.org/technologies/automated-license-plate-readers-alprs) cameras to track down a woman suspected of self-managing an abortion. The officer searched 6,809 different camera networks maintained by surveillance tech company [Flock Safety](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/02/anti-surveillance-mapmaker-refuses-flock-safetys-cease-and-desist-demand), including states where abortion access is protected by law, such as Washington and Illinois. The search record listed the reason plainly: “had an abortion, search for female.”
