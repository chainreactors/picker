## Brit retailer says troubled breakup with tech platform of former US owner nearing conclusion

Exclusive Two of the top team behind Asda's £1 billion ($1.31 billion) tech divorce from US retail giant Walmart — which has seen a number of setbacks — are departing the company.

![ASDA](https://regmedia.co.uk/2024/01/22/shutterstock_asda.jpg?x=174&amp;y=115&amp;crop=1)

Carl Dawson, Asda's CIO, and Rob Barnes, VP of digital and technology, are set to leave the UK's third largest supermarket, according to internal communications seen by *The Register*.

Dawson joined Asda from Marks & Spencer in April 2021, shortly after the company was [sold by Walmart](https://corporate.walmart.com/news/2021/02/16/issa-brothers-and-tdr-capital-complete-the-acquisition-of-asda-from-walmart) for £6.8 billion ($8.9 billion) to retail entrepreneurs Mohsin and Zuber Issa and private equity firm TDR Capital in February 2021.

Soon after, Asda signed a "transitional services agreement" with Walmart to guarantee support for IT systems it inherited for an initial period of three years.
