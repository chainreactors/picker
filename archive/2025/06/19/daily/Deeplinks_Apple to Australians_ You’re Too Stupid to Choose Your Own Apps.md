* [Español](/deeplinks/2025/06/apple-australians-youre-too-stupid-choose-your-own-apps?language=es)

Apple [has released a scaremongering, self-serving warning aimed at the Australian government](https://www.neowin.net/news/apple-warns-australia-against-joining-eu-in-mandating-iphone-app-sideloading/), claiming that Australians will be overrun by a parade of digital horribles if Australia follows the European Union’s lead and regulates Apple’s “walled garden.”

The [EU’s Digital Markets Act](https://www.eff.org/pages/adoption-dsadma-notre-analyse) is a big, complex, ambitious law that takes aim squarely at the source of Big Tech’s power: lock-in. For users, the DMA offers [interoperability rules](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/04/eu-digital-markets-acts-interoperability-rule-addresses-important-need-raises) that let Europeans escape US tech giants’ walled gardens [without giving up their relationships](https://www.eff.org/interoperablefacebook) and [digital memories](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2021/08/facebooks-secret-war-switching-costs).

For small businesses, the DMA offers something just as valuable: the right to process their own payments.
