## Accessing SSL certificate details in Safari 18.4 and later

April 14, 2025
[rtrouton](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/author/rtrouton/ "Posts by rtrouton") [Leave a comment](#respond)
[Go to comments](#comments)

In Safari 18.3 and earlier on macOS, it’s been possible to access details about SSL certificates used by the websites being visited using the following process:

1. Launch Safari if needed.

2. Click the address bar

3. Click the padlock icon on the left side of the address.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-13-at-10.11.27pm.png?w=599&h=497 "Screenshot 2025-04-13 at 10.11.27 PM.png")

4. Click the **View Certificate** button.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-13-at-10.11.39-pm.png?w=599&h=497 "Screenshot 2025-04-13 at 10.11.39  PM.png")

You should see a window with the SSL certificate details.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-13-at-10.11.42pm.png?w=599&h=497 "Screenshot 2025-04-13 at 10.11.42 PM.png")

In Safari 18.4 and later, this process has changed. The details are described in the [release notes for Safari 18.4](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/safari-release-notes/safari-18_4-release-notes):

***Added the ability to view certificate detail from Page Menu > more > Connection Security Details on iOS, iPadOS, and in visionOS, or Safari > Connection Security Details… on macOS. (139300381)***

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-1.53.png?w=600&h=251 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 1.53.png")

For more details, please see below the jump.

For Safari 18.4 and later for macOS, the process now looks like this:

1. Launch Safari if needed.

2. Click the **Safari** menu.

3. Select **Connection Security Details….**

**![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42.png?w=513&h=407 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.png")**

4. Click the **View Certificate** button.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-1.png?w=599&h=375 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.png")

You should see a window with the SSL certificate details.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-2.png?w=599&h=375 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.png")

For Safari 18.4 and later for iOS, the process now looks like this:

1. Launch Safari if needed.

2. Click the **Reader** icon.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-3.png?w=277&h=600 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.PNG")

3. Click on the ellipsis menu.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-4.png?w=277&h=600 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.PNG")

4. Click on **Connection Security Details**.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-5.png?w=277&h=600 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.PNG")

You should see a window with the SSL certificate details.

![](https://derflounder.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/screenshot-2025-04-14-at-2.42-6.png?w=277&h=600 "Screenshot 2025-04-14 at 2.42.PNG")
