* [Español](/deeplinks/2025/04/congress-takes-another-step-toward-enabling-broad-internet-censorship?language=es)

The House Energy and Commerce Committee on Tuesday [advanced](https://energycommerce.house.gov/calendars?start=2025-04-04&end=2025-04-11) the TAKE IT DOWN Act ([S. 146](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/146)) , a bill that seeks to speed up the removal of certain kinds of troubling online content. While the bill is meant to address a serious problem—the distribution of non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII)—the notice-and-takedown system it creates is an open invitation for powerful people to pressure websites into removing content they dislike.

As we’ve [written](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/03/trump-calls-congress-pass-overbroad-take-it-down-act-so-he-can-use-it-censor) [before](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/02/take-it-down-act-flawed-attempt-protect-victims-will-lead-censorship), while protecting victims of these heinous privacy invasions is a legitimate goal, good intentions alone are not enough to make good policy.
