### Quickpost: Testing A USB Fridge (Update)

Filed under: [Hardware](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/hardware/),[Quickpost](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/quickpost/) — Didier Stevens @ 0:00

I performed some extra tests with my USB fridge (see [Quickpost: Testing A USB Fridge](https://blog.didierstevens.com/2022/11/06/quickpost-testing-a-usb-fridge/)).

Here is how the temperature evolved when I put a can with cold water (around 12° C) in the USB fridge:

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/20221111-212120.png)

The temperature increased around 2° C over a period of 12 hours (room temperature was around 17 °C).

That required around 57 Wh.

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/screen04.bmp)

And the temperature at the top of the can increased more than at the bottom:

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/ir000525.jpg)

For reference, here is how the temperature evolves of a cooled can of water left on the desk in that same room (so not inside the USB fridge):

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/20221111-212047.png)
