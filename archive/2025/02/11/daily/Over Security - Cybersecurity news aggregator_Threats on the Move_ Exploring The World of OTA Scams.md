## Threats on the Move: Exploring The World of OTA Scams

![OTAs: Don't leave your customers frustrated and angry! Protect your external web assets from spoofing and impersonation!](https://bfore.ai/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Threats-on-the-Move-Best.png)

Leaving the excitement of the winter holidays behind, the turn of the new year often induces a spike in would-be travelers rushing to their favorite online travel platforms to plan their next adventure. As [the annual travel booking burst, or “wave season”](https://www.asta.org/travelerServices/travel-tips/travel-tips-articles/why-wave-season-matters) begins in January, bucket list experiences are planned by taking advantage of incredible deals offered by online travel agencies (OTAs). In case you aren’t familiar with the term, “OTA” is the abbreviation for “Online Travel Agency” and refers to travel companies that aggregate and sell travel products, services and packages online. Commonly-known OTAs include well-known brands such as [booking.com](http://booking.com), [Expedia](https://www.expedia.com/), [Tripadvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/), [Agoda](https://www.agoda.com/), [Airbnb](https://www.airbnb.com/), [Hotels.com](http://hotels.com), etc.
