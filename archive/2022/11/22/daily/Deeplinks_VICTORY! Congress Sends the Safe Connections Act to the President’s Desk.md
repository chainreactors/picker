In the 21st century, it is difficult to lead a life without a cell phone. It is also difficult to change your number—you’ve given it to all your friends, family, doctors, children’s schools, and so on. It’s especially difficult if you are trying to leave an abusive relationship where your abuser is in control of your family’s phone plan and therefore has access to your phone records.

Thankfully, Congress just passed a bill that will change that.

The Safe Connections Act ([S. 120](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/senate-bill/120/cosponsors?q=%7b%22search%22:%5b%22s.+120%22%5d%7d&r=1&s=1&searchResultViewType=expanded)) was introduced in January 2021 by Senators [Brian Schatz](https://www.schatz.senate.gov/news/press-releases/schatz-legislation-to-help-domestic-violence-survivors-cut-ties-with-abusers-separate-from-shared-wireless-service-plans-set-to-become-law), Deb Fischer, Richard Blumenthal, Rick Scott, and Jacky Rosen. It would make it easier for survivors of domestic violence to separate their phone line from a family plan while keeping their own phone number. It also requires the FCC to create rules to protect the privacy of the people seeking this protection. This bill overwhelmingly passed both chambers of Congress and was sent to the President’s desk on November 18, 2022.
