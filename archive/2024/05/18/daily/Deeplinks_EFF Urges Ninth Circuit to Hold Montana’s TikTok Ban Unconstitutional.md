Montana’s TikTok ban violates the First Amendment, EFF and others told the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals in a [friend-of-the-court](https://www.eff.org/document/alario-tiktok-v-knudsen-ca9-amicus-brief) brief and urged the court to affirm [a trial court’s holding](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/12/victory-montanas-unprecedented-tiktok-ban-unconstitutional) from December 2023 to that effect.

Montana’s ban ([which EFF and others opposed](https://www.aclu.org/documents/coalition-letter-opposing-montana-house-bill-that-would-ban-tiktok)) prohibits TikTok from operating anywhere within the state and imposes financial penalties on TikTok or any mobile application store that allows users to access TikTok. The district court [recognized](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.mtd.73544/gov.uscourts.mtd.73544.115.0_1.pdf) that Montana’s law “bans TikTok outright and, in doing so, it limits constitutionally protected First Amendment speech,” and blocked Montana’s ban from going into effect. Last year, EFF—along with the ACLU, Freedom of the Press Foundation, Reason Foundation, and the Center for Democracy and Technology—filed a [friend-of-the-court brief](https://www.aclu.org/documents/aclu-and-eff-amicus-brief-in-alario-tiktok-v-knudson) in support of TikTok and Montana TikTok users’ challenge to this law at the trial court level.
