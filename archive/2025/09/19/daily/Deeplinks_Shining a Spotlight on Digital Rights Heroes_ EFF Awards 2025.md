It's been a year full of challenges, but also important victories for digital freedoms. From EFF’s new lawsuit against [OPM and DOGE](https://www.eff.org/cases/american-federation-government-employees-v-us-office-personnel-management), to launching [Rayhunter](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/03/meet-rayhunter-new-open-source-tool-eff-detect-cellular-spying?language=en) (our new tool to detect cellular spying), to exposing the [censorship of abortion-related](https://www.eff.org/pages/our-stop-censoring-abortion-campaign-uncovers-social-media-censorship-crisis#main-content) content on social media, we’ve been busy! But we’re not the only ones leading the charge.

On September 10 in San Francisco, we presented the [annual EFF Awards](https://www.eff.org/event/eff-awards-2025) to three courageous honorees who are pushing back against unlawful surveillance, championing data privacy, and advancing civil liberties online. This year’s awards went to [Just Futures Law](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/08/eff-awards-spotlight-just-futures-law), [Erie Meyer](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/08/eff-awards-spotlight-erie-meyer), and the [Software Freedom Law Center, India](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/08/eff-awards-spotlight-software-freedom-law-center-india).
