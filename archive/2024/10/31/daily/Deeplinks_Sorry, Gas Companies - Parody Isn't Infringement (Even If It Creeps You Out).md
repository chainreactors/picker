Activism comes in many forms. You might [hold a rally](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2021/09/protestors-nationwide-rally-tell-apple-dont-break-encryption-dont-break-your), [write to Congress](https://act.eff.org/), or [fly a blimp](https://www.eff.org/press/releases/diverse-groups-fly-airship-over-nsas-utah-data-center-protest-illegal-internet-spying) over the NSA. *Or* you might use a [darkly hilarious parody](https://repaer.earth/) to make your point, like our client Modest Proposals recently did.

Modest Proposals is an activist collective that uses parody and culture jamming to advance environmental justice and other social causes. As part of a [campaign](https://www.themodestproposals.org/life-offsets.html) shining a spotlight on the environmental damage and human toll caused by the liquefied natural gas (LNG) industry, Modest Proposals invented a company called Repaer. The fake company’s [website](https://repaer.org/) offers energy companies the opportunity to purchase “life offsets” that balance the human deaths their activities cause by extending the lives of individuals deemed economically valuable.
