## Ad and cloud biz rubbishes claims that 183 million accounts broken into

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Carly Page](/Author/Carly-Page "Read more by this author")

Tue 28 Oct 2025  //
10:42 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

Panic spread faster than a phishing email on Tuesday after claims of a massive Gmail breach hit the headlines – but Google says it's all nonsense.

The ad giant moved quickly to quash reports that more than 183 million Gmail accounts had been compromised in a "major security breach." The claims, which appeared in numerous outlets including The Daily Mail, The Mirror, Forbes, The Independent, and the New York Post, are "false," according to Google, which blamed the fuss on a misunderstanding of old, recycled credentials rather than evidence of an intrusion.

![Ghostly figure hovering in a wooded glen](https://regmedia.co.uk/2019/08/06/shutterstock_ghost_in_theforest.jpg?x=174&amp;y=115&amp;crop=1)

The confusion appears to have started after Have I Been Pwned (HIBP) creator Troy Hunt [announced](https://www.troyhunt.com/inside-the-synthient-threat-data/) he had added a large dataset of 183 million credentials to the breach notification service. The data was shared with Hunt by Synthient, a threat intelligence platform that collects and analyzes information from infostealer malware logs.
