*Intern Nicholas Wilson was the primary author of this post.*

U.S. government intelligence agencies are buying data about us. The danger to our civil liberties is so extreme that even the Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) said things have gone too far in a detailed [report](https://www.odni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/assessments/ODNI-Declassified-Report-on-CAI-January2022.pdf) released in June.

The report explains that federal intelligence agencies, like the [NSA](https://www.eff.org/nsa-spying) and [FBI](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2021/07/should-congress-close-fbis-backdoor-spying-american-communications-yes), do more than just conduct their own surveillance: they also buy data from [private surveillance](https://www.eff.org/wp/behind-the-one-way-mirror) companies. This is a powerful partnership: the government’s desire to surveil us [aligns well](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/08/how-ad-tech-became-cop-spy-tech) with corporations’ incentives in the [surveillance economy](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/05/save-news-we-must-ban-surveillance-advertising).
