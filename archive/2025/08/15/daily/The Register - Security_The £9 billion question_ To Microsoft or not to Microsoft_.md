## Are UK taxpayers getting real value from SPA24 — or just high cost convenience?

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Bill McCluggage](/Author/Bill-McCluggage "Read more by this author")

07:30 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

Register debate series The UK government's five-year Strategic Partnership Agreement (SPA24) with Microsoft is set to see public sector bodies spend around £1.9 billion each year—nearly £9 billion in total over half a decade. It's a vast sum for software and services, and one that deserves close scrutiny.

![windows](https://regmedia.co.uk/2020/11/18/windows.jpg?x=174&amp;y=115&amp;crop=1)

### Microsoft in the public sector

This debate series is prompted by our reporting on the [recent UK government commitments to the tech giant](https://www.theregister.com/2025/08/07/uk_microsoft_spending/). [Yesterday's motion](https://www.theregister.com/2025/08/13/debate_for_microsoft_in_public_sector/) was for using Microsoft in the public sector. The debate continues with a motion for more oversight and balance between FOSS and the Windows maker today.

The key question is whether this represents genuine value for taxpayers — or whether it's simply the continuation of the status quo under a new label.
