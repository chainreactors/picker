## CEO of Cloud Software a 'special government employee' probing for Team Elon

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Jessica Lyons](/Author/Jessica-Lyons "Read more by this author")

Wed 5 Feb 2025  //
19:30 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

The US Treasury has revealed Tom Krause – the chief exec of Citrix and Netscaler owner Cloud Software Group – has "read-only" access to a vital federal government payment system that disburses trillions of dollars annually.

Krause, we're told, is a "special government employee" associated with Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency ([DOGE](https://www.theregister.com/2025/01/21/doge_us_digital_service_renamed/)) team, which had already gained access to that critical payment system among other federal computer systems. The group is currently rifling through contracts and programs across the board to shut down if deemed by Musk and co to be inefficient or inappropriate.

***FYI...*** Musk was instrumental in the looming shut down of Uncle Sam's USAID, which was coincidentally [being probed](https://gizmodo.com/elon-musks-enemy-usaid-was-investigating-starlink-over-its-contracts-in-ukraine-2000559365) by its inspector general over the agency's [monitoring](https://oig.usaid.gov/node/6814) of Ukraine's use of USAID-provided Starlink terminals.
