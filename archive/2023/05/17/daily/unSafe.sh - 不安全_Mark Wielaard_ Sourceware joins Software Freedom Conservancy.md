## Sourceware joins Software Freedom Conservancy

Posted on May 16, 2023, 16:12.

![Sourceware](https://gnu.wildebeest.org/blog/mjw/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2023/05/topbar-standalone.png)

![Software Freedom Conservancy](https://gnu.wildebeest.org/blog/mjw/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2022/09/conservancy-header.png)

After [various](https://sourceware.org/pipermail/overseers/2022q3/018804.html) [discussions](https://sourceware.org/pipermail/overseers/2022q3/018802.html) and lots of [positive](https://sourceware.org/pipermail/overseers/2022q3/018834.html) [feedback](https://www.fsf.org/events/sourceware-infrastructure-a-presentation-and-community-q-a) [Software Freedom Conservancy](https://sfconservancy.org/) and [Sourceware](https://sourceware.org/) proudly [announce](https://sfconservancy.org/news/2023/may/15/sourceware-joins-sfc/) that Sourceware today joins SFC as a member project!

For almost 25 years Sourceware has been the long-time home of various core toolchain project communities. Projects like Cygwin, a UNIX API for Win32 systems, the GNU Toolchain, including GCC, the GNU Compiler Colection, two C libraries, glibc and newlib, binary tools, binutils and elfutils, debuggers and profilers, GDB, systemtap and valgrind. Sourceware also hosts standard groups like gnu-gabi and the DWARF Debugging Standard. See the full list project hosted and services provided on the [Sourceware projects](https://sourceware.org/projects.html) page.

As the fiscal host of Sourceware, Software Freedom Conservancy will provide a home for fundraising, legal protection and governance that will benefit all projects under Sourceware’s care.  We share one mission: developing, distributing and advocating for Software Freedom.  Together we will offer a worry-free, friendly home for core toolchain and developer tool projects.
