* [Español](/deeplinks/2022/12/2022-year-review-state-online-free-expression-worldwide-2022?language=es)

It’s been a tumultuous year for free expression globally. From [internet shutdowns](https://www.accessnow.org/keepiton/), crackdowns on expression and [closed-door partnerships](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/08/online-platforms-should-stop-partnering-government-agencies-remove-content) to attempts to restrict anonymity and [end to end encryption](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/07/eff-and-partners-urge-indian-government-keep-end-end-encryption-alive), in many places, digital rights are under threat. And while the European Union has made [regulatory strides](https://www.eff.org/issues/eu-policy-principles), elsewhere in the world, efforts to regulate—particularly [those undertaken by authoritarian countries](https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-net/2022/countering-authoritarian-overhaul-internet)—threaten to fracture the global internet.

EFF is deeply engaged in the global fight for free expression online. In 2022, we worked with the [DSA Human Rights Alliance](https://www.eff.org/pages/dsa-human-rights-alliance) to ensure that EU lawmakers consider the global impacts of European legislation.
