# The Abortion Hotline Meta Wants to Go Dark

BY **[KENYATTA THOMAS](https://www.eff.org/about/staff/kenyatta-thomas)** | September 26, 2025

*This is the sixth installment in a blog series documenting EFF's findings from the* [*Stop Censoring Abortion*](https://www.eff.org/pages/stop-censoring-abortion) *campaign. You can read additional posts* [*here*](https://www.eff.org/pages/our-stop-censoring-abortion-campaign-uncovers-social-media-censorship-crisis#main-content)*.*

When we started our [Stop Censoring Abortion campaign](https://www.eff.org/pages/stop-censoring-abortion), we heard from activists, advocacy organizations, researchers, and even healthcare providers who had all experienced having abortion-related content removed or suppressed on social media. One of the submissions we received was from an organization called the Miscarriage and Abortion Hotline.

The [Miscarriage and Abortion Hotline](https://mahotline.org/) (M+A Hotline) formed in 2019, is staffed by a team of [healthcare providers who wanted to provide](https://mahotline.org/whoweare) free and confidential “expert advice on various aspects of miscarriage and abortion, ensuring individuals receive accurate information and compassionate support throughout their journey.” By 2022, the hotline was receiving between [25 to 45 calls and texts](https://www.everydayhealth.com/sexual-health/people-flocking-to-hotline-with-miscarriage-and-abortion-questions/) a day.
