# The Rising Threat of Cookie Theft and RMM Takeover

## Cookie theft and Remote Monitoring and Management (RMM) takeover have emerged as formidable challenges. These sophisticated tactics can compromise sensitive data, jeopardize user privacy, and even lead to financial losses. This article looks into the intricacies of cookie theft and RMM takeover, exploring their potential impact and offering insights into safeguarding your digital identity.

## Cookie Theft:

Cookies, small pieces of data stored on users’ devices by websites, play a crucial role in enhancing user experience. However, cybercriminals exploit vulnerabilities in this system through various methods, resulting in cookie theft. Once stolen, these cookies can be used to impersonate users, gaining unauthorized access to their accounts and sensitive information.

## Methods of Cookie Theft:

**1. Cross-Site Scripting (XSS):** Attackers inject malicious scripts into websites, compromising the security of the site and allowing them to steal cookies from users.

**2. Session Hijacking:** Cybercriminals intercept and hijack user sessions to gain access to sensitive data, including cookies, during an active session.
