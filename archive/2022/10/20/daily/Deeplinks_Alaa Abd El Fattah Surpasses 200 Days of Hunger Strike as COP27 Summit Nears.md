* [العربية](/deeplinks/2022/10/alaa-abd-el-fattah-surpasses-200-days-hunger-strike-cop27-summit-nears?language=ar)

We [remain](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/05/british-citizen-alaa-abd-el-fattah-demands-end-mistreatment-egyptian-prison) gravely concerned about the deteriorating health of Alaa Abd El Fattah, the British-Egyptian activist, technologist, 2022 [EFF Award winner](https://www.eff.org/awards/effawards/2022#:~:text=EFF's%20annual%20Pioneer%20Award%20Ceremony,has%20fused%20with%20modern%20life.), and Amnesty Prisoner of Conscience. Alaa has now been on hunger strike at Wadi el Natrun Prison in Egypt for more than 200 days, and was [reported this week](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/africa/egypt-cop27-cairo-alaa-abdelfattah-sisi-b2205797.html) as being “at death’s door” by the UK’s *Independent.*

World leaders, including UK Prime Minister Liz Truss, are set to gather soon in Egypt for the COP27 climate summit, despite the country’s ongoing crackdown on civil society, which UN experts have [criticized](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/us-politics/egypt-ap-cop27-red-sea-muslim-brotherhood-b2197994.html) as potentially jeopardizing “safety and full participation” at the summit.
