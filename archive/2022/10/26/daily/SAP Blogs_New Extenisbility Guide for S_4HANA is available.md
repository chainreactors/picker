I am happy to share with you the new ABAP extensibility guide for SAP S/4HANA in the cloud and on premise with you that was released today!

[Extend SAP S/4HANA in the cloud and on premise with ABAP based extensions](https://www.sap.com/documents/2022/10/52e0cd9b-497e-0010-bca6-c68f7e60039b.html)

Read the guide and find out how to create ABAP-based extension on SAP BTP ABAP Environment (aka Steampunk), SAP S/4HANA Cloud ABAP Environment or with Cloud ready developer extensibility in SAP S/4HANA any-premise or private cloud edition (aka Embedded Steampunk).

The new guide does not replace the existing guide [Custom Extensions in SAP S/4HANA Implementations – A Practical Guide for Senior IT Leadership](https://www.sap.com/documents/2020/03/ceeea71f-8a7d-0010-87a3-c30de2ffd8ff.html) , but it provides more focus and goes one level deeper on the ABAP-based extensions.

And in this context, the following blog post may be of interest for you, if you need an overview first:

[Steampunk is going all-in](https://blogs.sap.com/2021/09/30/steampunk-is-going-all-in/)

[How to use Embedded Steampunk in SAP S/4HANA Cloud, private edition and in on-premise – The new ABAP...](https://blogs.sap.com/2022/10/25/how-to-use-embedded-steampunk-in-sap-s-4hana-cloud-private-edition-and-in-on-premise-the-new-abap-extensibility-guide/)
