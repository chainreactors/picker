“[Pernicious](https://www.eff.org/document/05-23-2025-memorandum-opinion-jenner-block-lawsuit).”

“[Unprecedented... cringe-worthy](https://www.eff.org/document/05-02-2025-memorandum-opinion-perkins-coie-lawsuit).”

“[Egregious](https://www.eff.org/document/05-27-2025-memorandum-opinion-wilmerhale-lawsuit).”

“[Shocking](https://www.courthousenews.com/shocking-abuse-of-power-federal-judge-blocks-trump-retaliation-against-susman-godfrey/).”

These are just some of the words that federal judges used in recent weeks to describe President Trump’s politically motivated and vindictive executive orders targeting law firms that have employed people or represented clients or causes he doesn’t like.

But our favorite word by far is “unconstitutional.”

EFF was one of the very first legal organizations to publicly come out [in support of Perkins Coie](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/03/eff-stands-perkins-coie-and-rule-law) when it became the first law firm to challenge the legality of President Trump’s executive order targeting it. Since then, EFF has [joined four amicus briefs](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/04/eff-joins-amicus-brief-supporting-perkins-coie-law-firm-against-unconstitutional) in support of targeted law firms, and in all four cases, judges from the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia have indicated they’re having none of it.
