This International Women’s Day, defend free speech, fight surveillance, and support innovation by calling on our elected politicians and private companies to uphold our most fundamental rights—both online and offline.

### **1. [Pass the “My Body, My Data” Act](https://act.eff.org/action/pass-the-my-body-my-data-act-93a7c7d0-05da-4463-aa8a-a9a431ac2cbe)**

Privacy fears should never stand in the way of healthcare. That's why [this common-sense federal bill](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/05/eff-supports-my-body-my-data), sponsored by U.S. Rep. Sara Jacobs, will require businesses and non-governmental organizations to act responsibly with personal information concerning reproductive health care. Specifically, it restricts them from collecting, using, retaining, or disclosing reproductive health information that isn't essential to providing the service someone asks them for. The protected information includes data related to pregnancy, menstruation, surgery, [termination](https://www.fastcompany.com/90468030/how-an-online-search-for-abortion-pills-landed-this-woman-in-jail) of pregnancy, contraception, basal body temperature or diagnoses.
