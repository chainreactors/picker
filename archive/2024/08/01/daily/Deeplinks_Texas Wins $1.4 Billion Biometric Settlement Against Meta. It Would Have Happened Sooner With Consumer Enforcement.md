In Texasâ€™ first public enforcement of its biometric privacy law, Meta agreed to pay $1.4 billion to settle claims that its now-defunct face recognition system violated state law. The law was [first passed](https://lrl.texas.gov/legis/billsearch/BillDetails.cfm?legSession=77-0&billtypeDetail=HB&billNumberDetail=678&billSuffixDetail=) in 2001.

As part of the [Texas settlement](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/sites/default/files/images/press/Final%20State%20of%20Texas%20v%20Meta%20Order%202024.pdf), Meta (formerly Facebook) can seek pre-approval from the state for any future biometric projects. The settlement does not require Meta to destroy any models or algorithms trained on Texas biometric data, as the state urged in its [original lawsuit](https://texasattorneygeneral.gov/sites/default/files/images/child-support/State%20of%20Texas%20v.%20Meta%20Platforms%20Inc..pdf). And the settlement appears designed to avoid that remedy in the future.
