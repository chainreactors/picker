Austin organizers turned out to rebuke the city’s misguided contract with Flock Safety— and won. This successful pushback from the community means at the end of the month Austin police will no longer be able to use the surveillance network of automated license plate readers (ALPRs) across the city.

Two years ago [Austin City Council approved](https://www.kvue.com/article/news/police/austin-city-council-license-plate-readers/269-c86f788c-a27b-4c28-9084-829964d5ce3c) this controversial contract, despite strong local opposition. We knew then that these AI-driven surveillance systems weren’t just creepy, they are prone to [misuse](https://www.kwch.com/2022/10/31/kechi-police-lieutenant-arrested-using-police-technology-stalk-wife/) and [mistakes](https://centralcurrent.org/a-study-found-syracuses-new-license-plate-readers-make-frequent-mistakes-councilors-didnt-know-before-approving-them/) which have [a real human toll](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/11/human-toll-alpr-errors).

In the years since, this concern has materialized [time](https://www.santafenewmexican.com/news/local_news/sisters-sue-espa-ola-over-traffic-stop-they-say-was-illegal/article_1c949c30-ae3e-11ee-ad85-f71a8c9ecb1e.html) [and](https://www.newsobserver.com/news/state/north-carolina/article287381160.html) [time](https://www.freep.com/story/news/local/michigan/detroit/2024/09/16/detroit-police-license-plate-readers-isoke-robinson-car-shooting/75189126007/) [again](https://www.almanacnews.com/police/2024/02/12/atherton-officer-involved-in-the-arrest-of-m-a-student-also-named-in-lawsuit-over-unreasonable-seizure-and...
