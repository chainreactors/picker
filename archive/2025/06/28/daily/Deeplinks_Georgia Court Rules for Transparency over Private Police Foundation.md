* [Español](/deeplinks/2025/06/georgia-court-rules-transparency-over-private-police-foundation?language=es)

A [Georgia court has decided](https://atlpresscollective.com/2025/06/02/acpc-lpl-open-records-apf/) that private non-profit [Atlanta Police Foundation](https://atlpresscollective.com/tag/atlanta-police-foundation/) (APF) must comply with public records requests under the Georgia Open Records Act for some of its functions on behalf of the Atlanta Police Department. This is a major win for transparency in the state.

 The [lawsuit was brought last year](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/03/lucy-parsons-labs-takes-police-foundation-court-open-records-requests) by the Atlanta Community Press Collective (ACPC) and [Electronic Frontier Alliance](https://efa.eff.org) member Lucy Parsons Labs (LPL). It concerns the APF’s refusal to disclose records about its role as the leaser and manager of the site of so-called Cop City, the Atlanta Public Safety Training Center at the heart of a years-long battle that pitted local social and environmental movements against the APF.
