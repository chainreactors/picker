## About Primark

Consumer retail brands that enjoy robust online traffic are prime targets for impersonation attacks (when criminals use a brand’s name and likeness to fool and lure their customers). By offering deceptive too-good-to-be-true “deals”, special “offers”, and last-minute “sales”, well-known consumer brands consistently rank among some of the most impersonated.

Primark, an Ireland-based fashion, home, and beauty retailer with a global footprint, has undergone a substantial digital transformation in recent years. Since 2020, Primark’s digital presence has grown from a basic website featuring less than 15% of in-store products to over 80%. In addition, Primark’s web traffic has tripled and their digital team has grown in parallel as store stock-checker functionality was added in all markets, as well as a thriving click-and-collect business in the UK.
