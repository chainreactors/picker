# The Bug Bounty Program Litmus Test: 5 Red Flags to Avoid Wasting Your Time

[![Andrei Ivan](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fill:64:64/1*ajsLg708YNtjFmUSN4yL2g.png)](https://medium.com/%40sync-with-ivan?source=post_page---byline--1adc0034de43---------------------------------------)

Bug bounty hunters live by one scarce resource: time. Spending weeks on a program that pays pennies, ignores reports, or buries you in duplicates is the fastest route to burnout. Use this five-point litmus test to spot the duds before they drain your motivation, and focus your energy on programs that actually reward your skill.

> If you’re new to bug bounty hunting and want a breakdown of the major platforms of bug bounty programs, check out my guide to bug bounty platforms [Popular Bug Bounty Platforms for Beginners: Your Complete Guide to Getting Started](https://medium.com/%40sync-with-ivan/popular-bug-bounty-platforms-for-beginners-your-complete-guide-to-getting-started-22eb72f3f63f)

## 1. VDP-Only or Rock-Bottom Payouts

A “Vulnerability Disclosure Program” (VDP) with no cash rewards is fine for warm-ups or tooling practice, but it will never fund your rent. Mature hunters treat VDP-only targets like a gym: a few reps, then move on. Even paid programs can wave a red flag when critical‐severity bounties top out below $500… especially if the company’s revenue sits in the billions.

**Quick check**

* Scan the payout grid → critical < $1 000? Skip.
* Compare to sector averages (FinTech/Web3 regularly pay 5–10× more).
* Watch hacktivity: low average bounty = low priority for the company.
