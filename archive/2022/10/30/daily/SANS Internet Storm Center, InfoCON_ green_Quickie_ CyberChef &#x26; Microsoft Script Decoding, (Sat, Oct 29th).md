**Published**: 2022-10-29. **Last Updated**: 2022-10-29 14:07:37 UTC

[0 comment(s)](/diary/Quickie%2BCyberChef%2BMicrosoft%2BScript%2BDecoding/29198/#comments)

This week I discovered a CyberChef operation I didn't know existed, but I'm [quite familiar](https://blog.didierstevens.com/2016/03/29/decoding-vbe/) with the algorythm: "[Microsoft Script Decoder](https://github.com/gchq/CyberChef/commit/4a86340d501a9e1acc72185f81f98bdb4911cb84)" (it's been there at least since 2017).

JScript and VBScript scripts can be encoded, and are usually given the extensions .jse or .vbe respectively.

CyberChef can decode these scripts:
