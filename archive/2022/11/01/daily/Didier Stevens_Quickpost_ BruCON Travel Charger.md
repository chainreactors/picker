# [Didier Stevens](https://blog.didierstevens.com/)

## Monday 31 October 2022

### Quickpost: BruCON Travel Charger

Filed under: [Hardware](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/hardware/),[Quickpost](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/quickpost/) — Didier Stevens @ 0:00

In my [BruCON](https://www.brucon.org/2022/) speaker goodie bag, I found a travel adapter & USB charger:

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/20221023-111047.png)

I already have a similar travel adapter, but this BruCON travel adapter has one extra important feature for me: a USB C port.

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/20221203-124313.png)

As I still had my setup ready for [testing the electrical energy consumption](https://blog.didierstevens.com/2022/07/12/quickpost-standby-power-consumption-of-my-usb-chargers/) of devices, I quickly tested the standby power of this charger.

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/screen37.bmp)

It’s average standby electrical power consumption is 236,46 mW. Standby means: I plug the adapter into an electrical outlet (230V) without connecting any device for charging.

I imagine that for a travel adapter, standby consumption is not that important, as one would use it only occasionally.
