## Tulsi Gabbard boasts Washington forced Blighty to drop iPhone encryption fight

![icon](/design_picker/d518b499f8a6e2c65d4d8c49aca8299d54b03012/graphics/icon/vulture_red.svg)
[Carly Page](/Author/Carly-Page "Read more by this author")

Tue 19 Aug 2025  //
09:17 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

The UK government has reportedly abandoned its attempt to strong-arm Apple into weakening iPhone encryption after the White House forced Blighty into a quiet climb-down.

![barr](https://regmedia.co.uk/2019/07/23/barr1.jpg?x=174&amp;y=115&amp;crop=1)

US Director of National Intelligence [Tulsi Gabbard broke the news on X](https://x.com/DNIGabbard/status/1957623737232007638), boasting that she'd been working "closely with our partners in the UK, alongside @POTUS and @VP, to ensure Americans' private data remains private and our Constitutional rights and civil liberties are protected."

"As a result," she added, "the UK has agreed to drop its mandate for Apple to provide a 'backdoor' that would have enabled access to the protected encrypted data of American citizens and encroached on our civil liberties."

The Home Office has not yet confirmed the news, though a UK official told [The Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/ab0aba27-81e0-4ee5-bcbb-6bce85386e40) that "we can't and we won't" force Apple to provide a backdoor for encrypted data.
