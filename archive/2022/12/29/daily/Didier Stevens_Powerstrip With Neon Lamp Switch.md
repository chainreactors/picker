### Powerstrip With Neon Lamp Switch

Filed under: [Elec](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/elec/),[Hardware](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/hardware/) — Didier Stevens @ 0:00

There are [powerstrips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_strip) with a switch that lights up when the switch is turned on. Like this one:

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/20221224-121730.png)

These switches (certainly older models) often use a [neon lamp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neon_lamp) as light source.

I measured the electric energy consumption of a powerstrip with switch on and neon lamp burning (without anything plugged into the powerstrip’s outlets).

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/20221224-121933.png)
![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/20221224-121941.png)

It consumed 7,8582 Wh over 24 hours, thus it drew on average 0,327 W.

That’s about 6 times more than the [standby power of my Apple USB charger A2347](https://blog.didierstevens.com/2022/07/25/quickpost-standby-power-consumption-of-my-usb-chargers-120v-vs-230v/) (0,0530 W).

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/20221224-122729.png)

FYI: although the switch is turned on in the above picture, you don’t see the neon lamp burning.

That’s because of the [AC power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternating_current) here in Belgium is 230V and 50Hz.

50Hz means that the current is 0 A 100 times per second, and thus the neon lamp does not light up around these 0 A current values.

So the picture above was taken at a moment that the lamp wasn’t lighting up because the current was (almost) 0 A.

I will go into more details in an upcoming blog post.

I did not conduct tests with powerstrips that use LEDs in stead of neon lamps yet, because all the powerstrips with LEDs I have, also have a builtin USB charger, and that draws power too.
