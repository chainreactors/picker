# What Made the 90’s So Awesome?

February 6, 2023

[#reading](/archives/?tag=reading) [#culture](/archives/?tag=culture) [#society](/archives/?tag=society) [#technology](/archives/?tag=technology) [#top](/archives/?tag=top)

![](/images/f7a56604-935d-4bcb-a173-892bb442c8f2-miessler-90s-MJ.png)

I just read [a brilliant essay about the 90’s](https://freddiedeboer.substack.com/p/its-so-sad-when-old-people-romanticize?utm_source=danielmiessler.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=what-made-the-90-s-so-awesome) > by [Freddie de Boer](https://freddiedeboer.substack.com/?utm_source=danielmiessler.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=what-made-the-90-s-so-awesome) >, and it got me thinking. What made the 90’s so great?

Here’s GPT’s answer:

Give a 90’s lover’s view of what made the 90’s awesome. Include everything from parenting, art, entertainment, games, childhood, movies, TV, news, culture, and other stuff. Make a bulleted list with 30 words max per bullets, organized by category, with 5 items per category. Write each bullet as a statement of how awesome it was.

Parenting:

* Encouragement of creativity and self-expression
* Focus on quality family time
* Emphasis on good manners and respect
* Encouragement of independence and problem-solving skills
* Promotion of outdoor play and exploration

Art:

* The rise of alternative and grunge music
* The popularity of abstract and minimalist art
* The emergence of hip hop and rap as mainstream genres
* The explosion of street art and graffiti
* The influence of technology on art and design

Entertainment:

* The popularity of sitcoms like Friends and Seinfeld
* The rise of reality TV shows like The Real World
* The emergence of boy bands and girl groups
* The popularity of video game consoles like the PlayStation and Nintendo 64
* The success of blockbuster movies like Titanic and Jurassic Park

Childhood:

* The popularity of toys like Beanie Babies and Tamagotchi
* The rise of children’s TV shows like Rugrats and Power Rangers
* The popularity of book series like Goosebumps and The Baby-Sitters Club
* The emergence of educational computer games like Oregon Trail and Math Blaster
* The popularity of outdoor activities like rollerblading and skateboarding

Culture:

* The rise of environmentalism and eco-consciousness
* The popularity of grunge and alternative fashion
* The emergence of the internet and the beginning of the digital age
* The influence of hip hop culture on fashion and language
* The popularity of coffee culture and the rise of Starbucks

News:

* The fall of the Berlin Wall and the end of the Cold War
* The rise of the internet and the beginning of the information age
* The impeachment of President Bill Clinton
* The launch of the Hubble Space Telescope
* The first Gulf War and the rise of 24-hour news networks

Games:

* The popularity of trading card games like Magic: The Gathering and Pokemon
* The emergence of first-person shooter games like Doom and GoldenEye 007
* The popularity of fighting games like Street Fighter and Mortal Kombat
* The rise of multiplayer online games like Ultima Online and EverQuest
* The success of puzzle games like Myst and Tetris

Movies:

* The success of animated films like The Lion King and Toy Story
* The popularity of action movies like The Matrix and Terminator 2
* The emergence of independent films like Pulp Fiction and The Big Lebowski
* The success of romantic comedies like Pretty Woman and Sleepless in Seattle
* The popularity of teen movies like Clueless and American Pie

TV:

* The popularity of dramas like The X-Files and ER
* The success of animated shows like The Simpsons and South Park
* The emergence of cable networks like HBO and their original programming
* The popularity of talk shows like The Oprah Winfrey Show and The Jerry Springer Show
* The success of sci-fi shows like Star Trek: The Next Generation and Babylon 5

Other Stuff:

* The popularity of fitness trends like Tae Bo and step aerobics
* The emergence of extreme sports like snowboarding and BMX
* The rise of home shopping networks like QVC and the Home Shopping Network
* The popularity of infomercials and their products like the George Foreman Grill and the ThighMaster
* The success of self-help reading like The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People and Men Are from Mars, Women Are from Venus

Now tell me GPT doesn’t "understand" the 90’s.
