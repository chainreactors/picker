![2024 Year In Review Banner](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/2024-review-1c.jpg?itok=Pb1Gk62y)

We've been fighting [against](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2021/07/victory-fourth-circuit-rules-baltimores-warrantless-aerial-surveillance-program) [aerial surveillance](https://www.eff.org/press/releases/us-drone-test-sites-must-include-stronger-privacy-protections) for [decades](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2012/07/faa-releases-thousands-pages-drone-records) because we recognize the immense threat from Big Brother in the sky. Even if you’re behind within the confines of your backyard, you are exposed to [eyes from above](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/10/cop-companies-want-all-your-data-and-other-takeaways-years-iacp-conference).

Aerial surveillance was first conducted with manned aircrafts, which the Supreme Court held was permissible without a warrant in a [couple](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/476/207/) of [cases](https://www.oyez.org/cases/1988/87-764) the 1980s. But, as we’ve [argued](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/09/eff-michigan-court-governments-shouldnt-be-allowed-use-drone-spy-you-without) to courts, drones have changed the equation. Drones were a technology developed by the military before it was adopted by domestic law enforcement. And in the past decade, commercial drone makers began marketing to civilians, making drones [ubiquitous](https://www.statista.com/topics/7939/drones/#topicOverview) in our lives and exposing us to be watched by from above by the [government](https://atlasofsurveillance.org/search?location=&technologies%5Bdrones%5D=on&sort=) and our neighbors.
