On [January 31](https://twitter.com/olabini/status/1620616329375150081), the Swedish free software developer and computer security expert Ola Bini was [declared innocent](https://peoplesdispatch.org/2023/02/01/digital-rights-activist-ola-bini-declared-innocent-by-ecuadorian-court/) in a unanimous verdict by a three-judge tribunal in Ecuador—the Court of Criminal Guarantees of Pichincha. After almost four years of a criminal prosecution [plagued with](https://www.eff.org/tags/ola-bini) irregularities, delays, and due process violations, his right to a fair trial has finally prevailed. [EFF](https://twitter.com/EFF/status/1620810801563975683), [Access Now](https://twitter.com/accessnow_latam/status/1620783009338363904), [APC](https://www.apc.org/es/news/ola-bini-declarado-inocente-este-fallo-judicial-ayuda-que-ecuador-sea-un-pais-mejor-para-la), [Fundación Karisma](https://twitter.com/Karisma/status/1620837940929916928), and other digital and human rights groups that have been monitoring the [case](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/05/eff-and-other-civil-society-organizations-issue-report-danger-digital-rights-what) celebrate the ruling.
