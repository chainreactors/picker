# A year of SANS security summits

## A mixture of free online events and in-person conferences that put you at the heart of the cyber security industry

[David Gordon](/Author/David-Gordon "Read more by this author")

Mon 24 Oct 2022  //
09:11 UTC

![](/design_picker/d2e337b97204af4aa34dda04c4e5d56d954b216f/graphics/icons/social_share_icon.svg)

Sponsored Post Where do the world's cyber security professionals get an opportunity to mingle and swap tips with their global peers while engaging in interactive, hands-on learning exercises that will help them stop cyber criminals in their tracks?

That's the aim of the SANS Summits being held over the next few months, covering everything from pen testing and red/blue/purple team simulation to locking down the Internet of Things (IoT) and Industrial Control System (ICS) devices and how best to make use of threat intelligence to predict what hackers are likely to do next.

Here's a quick summary of what's on:

### The Pen Test HackFest Summit: 14 – 15 November, US and Virtual

Features the latest research in pen testing, exploit writing, red teaming and adversary emulation, with experienced cyber security professionals sharing their tried and trusted techniques, tools, and capabilities. Head [here to browse the full agenda](https://www.sans.org/u/1nm6).

### CyberThreat22: 16 – 17 January 2023, London and virtual

SANS is joining forces with the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) to deliver a series of presentations from top global cyber security experts with an emphasis on hands-on techniques and real-world deployments designed to boost your knowledge and skills. [The event](https://www.sans.org/u/1nmb) also offers the opportunity to take part in the CyberThreat Capture The Flag (CTF) challenge where you can pit your wits either against, or alongside, your colleagues and friends.
