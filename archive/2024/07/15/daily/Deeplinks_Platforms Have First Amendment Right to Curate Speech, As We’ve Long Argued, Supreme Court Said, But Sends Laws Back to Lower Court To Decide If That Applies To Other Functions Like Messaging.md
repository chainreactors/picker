Social media platforms, at least in their most common form, have a First Amendment right to curate the third-party speech they select for and recommend to their users, and the government’s ability to dictate those processes is extremely limited, the U.S. Supreme Court [stated](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/23pdf/22-277diff_3e04.pdf) in its landmark decision in [*Moody v. NetChoice*](https://www.scotusblog.com/case-files/cases/moody-v-netchoice-llc/) [and](https://www.eff.org/cases/netchoice-must-carry-litigation?page=1) [*NetChoice v. Paxton*](https://www.scotusblog.com/case-files/cases/netchoice-llc-v-paxton/)*,* which were decided together.
