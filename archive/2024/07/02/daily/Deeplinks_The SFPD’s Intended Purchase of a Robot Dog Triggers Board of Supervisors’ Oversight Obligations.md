The San Francisco Police Department (SFPD) [wants to get a robot quadruped](https://missionlocal.org/2024/06/sfpd-considers-robot-dog-spot/), popularly known as a robot dog. The city’s Board of Supervisors has a regulatory duty to probe into this intended purchase, including potentially blocking it altogether.

The SFPD recently proposed the acquisition of a new robot dog in a [report](https://www.sanfranciscopolice.org/sites/default/files/2024-06/SFPD_AB481_2024AnnualReport_20240611.pdf) about the department’s existing military arsenal and its proposed future expansion. The particular model that SFPD claims they are exploring, [Boston Dynamics’s Spot](https://bostondynamics.com/products/spot/), is capable of intrusion and surveillance in a manner similar to drones and other unmanned vehicles and is able to hold “payloads” like cameras.
