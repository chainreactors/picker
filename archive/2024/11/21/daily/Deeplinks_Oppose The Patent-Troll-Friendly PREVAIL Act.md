**Update 11/21/2024:**The Senate Judiciary Committee voted 11-10 in favor of [PREVAIL,](https://www.coons.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/prevail_act.pdf) and several senators expressed concerns about the bill. Thanks to EFF supporters who spoke out! We will continue to oppose this misguided bill.

Good news: the Senate Judiciary Committee has dropped one of the two terrible patent bills it was considering, the patent-troll-enabling [Patent Eligibility Restoration Act (PERA)](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/09/bill-would-boost-worst-patents-software-and-human-genes).

Bad news: the committee is still pushing the [PREVAIL](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/11/publics-right-fight-bad-patents-must-be-protected) Act, a bill that would hamstring the U.S.’s most effective system for invalidating bad patents. PREVAIL is a windfall for patent trolls, and Congress should reject  it.

[Take Action](https://act.eff.org/action/tell-congress-we-can-t-afford-to-bring-back-the-worst-patents/)

[Tell Congress: No New Bills For Patent Trolls](https://act.eff.org/action/tell-congress-we-can-t-afford-to-bring-back-the-worst-patents/)

One of the most effective tools to fight bad patents in the U.S. is a little-known but important system called inter partes review, or IPR. Created by Congress in 2011, the IPR process addresses a major problem: too many invalid patents slip through the cracks at the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office.
