Independent journalists increasingly gather newsworthy information and publish it on social media, often without the involvement of traditional news media. They make important contributions to public discourse and are often the first to [report newsworthy events](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2014/aug/16/fergusons-citizen-journalists-video). Courts must scrupulously safeguard their First Amendment rights to gather and publish the news. (Sometimes they are called “citizen journalists,” but of course, many independent journalists are non-citizens.)

EFF this week filed an [amicus brief](https://www.eff.org/document/2022-12-12-villarreal-v-laredo-5c-amicus-br-eff-nppa-pipp) arguing that when police officers wrongly arrest an independent journalist in violation of the First Amendment, courts must order the officers to pay damages. The brief was written by [Covington](https://www.cov.com/), and our co-amici are the [National Press Photographers Association](https://nppa.org/) and the [Pelican Institute](https://pelicanpolicy.org/).
