Community members [coordinated](https://www.instagram.com/p/DFs1PRAxA4z/) to pack Little Rock City Hall on Tuesday, where board members voted 5-3 to [end](https://katv.com/news/local/little-rock-approves-1-million-for-community-programs-gunshot-detection-service-fails-the-city-of-little-rock-has-approved-1-million-for-positive-prevention-programming-as-well-as-voted-no-to-a-contract-renewal-of-the-wide-area-gunshot-detection-service) the city's contract with ShotSpotter.

Initially funded through a [federal grant](https://www.apnews.com/article/09b740a206f34a42a26c532398160b10), Little Rock began its experiment with the “gunshot detection” sensors in 2018. ShotSpotter (now SoundThinking) has long been accused of [steering federal grants](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/shotspotter-police-gunshot-technology-federal-grants-rcna13815) toward local police departments in an effort to [secure funding for the technology](https://www.soundthinking.com/blog/grants-for-public-safety-a-comprehensive-guide/). Members of Congress are [investigating](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/05/shots-fired-congressional-letter-questions-dhs-funding-shotspotter) this funding. EFF has long encouraged communities to [follow the money](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2020/09/how-police-fund-surveillance-technology-part-problem) that pays for police surveillance technology.
