![Text "Pride 2023" over a pixel grid. "Pride" rainbow flag, 2023 black brown trans and intersex flags](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/pride-2023-b.png?itok=G4AOtdq5 "Pride 2023")

A growing number of [states](https://www.lgbtmap.org/equality-maps/healthcare/youth_medical_care_bans) [have](https://www.kff.org/other/issue-brief/youth-access-to-gender-affirming-care-the-federal-and-state-policy-landscape/) [prohibited](https://www.cnn.com/2023/06/06/politics/states-banned-medical-transitioning-for-transgender-youth-dg/index.html) [transgender](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/05/17/us/texas-transgender-care-ban-children.html) [youths](https://www.hrc.org/resources/attacks-on-gender-affirming-care-by-state-map) from obtaining gender-affirming health care. Some of these states are also restricting access by [transgender](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/04/22/health/transgender-adults-treatment-bans.html) [adults](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/health/missouri-governor-signs-bill-banning-gender-affirming-care-for-minors-and-some-adults). Fortunately, other states have responded by enacting [sanctuary](https://www.lgbtmap.org/equality-maps/healthcare/trans_shield_laws) [laws](https://apnews.com/article/abortion-transgender-shield-laws-washington-3ee92fd1f821319246313a463edac774) to protect trans people who visit to obtain this health care.

Even before these new laws, most trans people had to [travel out of state](https://newsroom.uw.edu/news/many-trans-patients-must-travel-out-state-surgery) to obtain gender-affirming surgery. After these laws, we can expect much more out-of-state travel for all forms of gender-affirming health care.

To be the most welcoming health care sanctuary, a pro-trans state must also be a *data sanctuary*.
