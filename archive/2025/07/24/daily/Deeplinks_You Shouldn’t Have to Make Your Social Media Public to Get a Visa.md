* [Español](/deeplinks/2025/07/you-shouldnt-have-make-your-social-media-public-get-visa?language=es)

The Trump administration is [continuing](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/04/trump-administrations-targeting-international-students-jeopardizes-free-speech-and) [its](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/06/eff-department-homeland-security-no-social-media-surveillance-immigrants) [dangerous push](https://www.justsecurity.org/114607/how-dhss-new-social-media-vetting-policies-threaten-free-speech/) to surveil and suppress foreign students’ social media activity. The State Department recently [announced](https://www.state.gov/releases/office-of-the-spokesperson/2025/06/announcement-of-expanded-screening-and-vetting-for-visa-applicants/) an unprecedented new requirement that applicants for student and exchange visas must set all social media accounts to “public” for government review. The State Department [also indicated](https://time.com/7295949/international-student-visas-colleges-universities-social-media-state-department-trump/) that if applicants refuse to unlock their accounts or otherwise don’t maintain a social media presence, the government may interpret it as an attempt to evade the requirement or deliberately hide online activity.
