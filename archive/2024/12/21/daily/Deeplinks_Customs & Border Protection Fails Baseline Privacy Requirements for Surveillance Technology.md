U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has failed to address six out of six main privacy protections for three of its border surveillance programs—surveillance towers, aerostats, and unattended ground sensors—according to [a new assessment by the Government Accountability Office](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-25-107302.pdf) (GAO).

In the report, GAO compared the policies for these technologies against six of the key Fair Information Practice Principles that agencies are supposed to use when evaluating systems and processes that may impact privacy, as dictated by both Office of Management and Budget [guidance](https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/sites/default/files/omb/assets/OMB/circulars/a130/a130revised.pdf) and the Department of Homeland Security's own [rules](https://www.dhs.gov/sites/default/files/2024-01/Fair%20Information%20Principles_12_2008.pdf).

![A chart of the various technologies and how they comply with FIPS](/files/2024/12/20/gao_charted_highlighted.png "A chart of the various technologies and how they comply with FIPS")
