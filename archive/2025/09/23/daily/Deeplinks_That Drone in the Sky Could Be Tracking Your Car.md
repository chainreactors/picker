![Several spying drones with eyeballs](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/drone-spy-3.jpg?itok=9D-H4RSy)

Police are using their drones as flying automated [license plate readers](https://sls.eff.org/technologies/automated-license-plate-readers-alprs) (ALPRs), airborne police cameras that make it easier than ever for law enforcement to follow you.

"The Flock Safety drone, specifically, are flying LPR cameras as well,” [Rahul Sidhu](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rahulsidhu?trk=public_post_feed-actor-name), Vice President of Aviation at Flock Safety, recently told a group of potential law enforcement customers interested in [drone-as-first-responder (DFR) programs](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/06/drone-first-responder-programs-are-latest-aerial-police-surveillance-push).

The integration of Flock Safety’s [flagship ALPR technology with its Aerodome drone equipment](https://www.flocksafety.com/blog/flock-safety-expands-into-drones-for-law-enforcement-with-acquisition-of-aerodome) is a police surveillance combo poised to elevate the privacy threats to civilians caused by both of these invasive technologies as drone adoption expands.
