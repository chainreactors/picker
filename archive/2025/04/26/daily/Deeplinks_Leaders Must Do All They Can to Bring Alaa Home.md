![Drawing of Laila Souief, mother of Alaa Abd el-Fattah](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/alaa-art-2-aa.png?itok=Y-0l5jpv)

It has now been nearly two months since UK Prime Minister Starmer [spoke with](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/pm-call-with-president-of-egypt-abdel-fattah-el-sisi-28-february-2025) Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi, yet there has been no tangible progress in the case of Alaa Abd El Fattah, the British-Egyptian writer, activist, and technologist who remains [imprisoned in Egypt](https://www.eff.org/offline/alaa-abd-el-fattah).

In yet another blow to his family and supporters, who have been [tirelessly advocating](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/11/alaa-abd-el-fattahs-43rd-birthday-fight-his-release-continues) for his release, we’ve now learned that Alaa has [fallen ill](https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/alaa-abd-el-fattah-falls-ill-after-marking-50-days-hunger-strike) while on a sustained hunger strike protesting his incarceration. Alaa’s sentence was due to end last September.

Alaa’s mother, Laila Soueif, initiated a hunger strike beginning on his intended release date to amplify demands for her son’s release. Soueif, too, is [facing deteriorating health](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2025/02/uk-egypt-mothers-hunger-strike-for-imprisoned-activist-son-alaa-abd-el-fattah-could-be-a-matter-of-life-and-death/), having to shift from a full hunger strike to a partial strike allowing for 300 liquid calories a day after being hospitalized in London, and following Starmer’s subsequent call with el-Sisi.
