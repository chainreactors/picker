# [Veeam Phishing via Wav File](/forums/diary/Veeam%2BPhishing%2Bvia%2BWav%2BFile/32120/)

**Published**: 2025-07-18. **Last Updated**: 2025-07-18 07:39:05 UTC
**by** [Xavier Mertens](/handler_list.html#xavier-mertens) (Version: 1)

[0 comment(s)](/diary/Veeam%2BPhishing%2Bvia%2BWav%2BFile/32120/#comments)

A interesting phishing attempt was reported by a contact. It started with a simple email that looked like a voice mail notification like many VoIP systems deliver when the call is missed. There was a WAV file attached to the mail[[1](https://blog.rootshell.be/stuff/veeam-voicemsg.wav)].

Here is a transcript of the recording:

"*Hi, this is xxxx from Veeam Software. I'm calling you today regarding ... <not clear> ... your backup license which has expired this month. Would you please give me a call to discuss about it?*"

This was not targeted because the person who received the mail was not involved with Veeam (or any IT environment). Did you receive such emails recently or in the past?
