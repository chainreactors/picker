We all should have the ability to have a private conversation, and it follows that we need ways to communicate privately online as well. In the digital world, end-to-end encryption is our best chance to maintain our privacy and security.

In the fraught legal landscape following [the U.S. Supreme Court’s](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/06/effs-statement-dobbs-abortion-ruling) [*Dobbs*](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/06/effs-statement-dobbs-abortion-ruling) [decision](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/06/effs-statement-dobbs-abortion-ruling), digital privacy around reproductive health has become critical. Several states are already enforcing abortion bans, and the Brennan Center for Justice has noted [more than 100 state bills that were introduced in 2022 to further limit abortion access](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/60-days-after-dobbs-state-legal-developments-abortion). At the same time, many states, including California and New York, have moved to protect or expand the right to abortion access, including for out-of-state persons. In this month’s elections, voters in California, Michigan, and Vermont [enshrined the right to abortion in their state constitutions](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/11/09/us/abortion-rights-ballot-proposals.html).
