*This is part two of a series. Part one on surveillance and control around the July election is [here](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/09/unveiling-venezuelas-repression-surveillance-and-censorship-following-julys).*

Over the past decade, the government in Venezuela has meticulously constructed a [framework of surveillance](https://www.reuters.com/investigates/special-report/venezuela-zte/) and repression, which has been [repeatedly denounced](https://www.accessnow.org/press-release/open-letter-technology-enabled-political-violence-venezuela/) by civil society and digital rights defenders in the country. This apparatus is built on a foundation of [restricted access to information](https://rsf.org/en/who-will-cover-venezuela-s-elections-press-freedom-disintegrates-under-maduro-s-regime), censorship, harassment of journalists, and the [closure of media outlets](https://latamjournalismreview.org/articles/closure-of-400-media-outlets-in-20-years-aggravates-unemployment-crisis-in-venezuelan-journalism/).
