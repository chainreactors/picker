![An NSA employee sits at a screen monitoring international communications](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/nationalsecurity_banner-2_0.png?itok=phfodlBe "Section 702")

A bombshell report from [WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/fbi-section-702-us-person-queries-email/) reveals that two days after the [U.S. Congress renewed and expanded](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/04/us-senate-and-biden-administration-shamefully-renew-and-expand-fisa-section-702-0) the mass-surveillance authority [Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act](https://www.eff.org/702-spying), the deputy director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), Paul Abbate, sent an email imploring agents to “*use*” Section 702 to search the communications of Americans collected under this authority “to demonstrate why tools like this are essential” to the FBI’s mission.

In other words, an agency that has [repeatedly](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/07/deja-vu-fbi-proves-again-it-cant-be-trusted-section-702) [abused](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/04/internal-documents-show-how-little-fbi-did-correct-misuse-section-702-databases) [this](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/05/newly-public-fisc-opinion-best-evidence-why-congress-must-end-section-702) [exact](https://www.eff.org/document/october-3-2011-fisc-opinion-holding-nsa-surveillance-unconstitutional) authority—with [*3.4 million*](https://www.dni.gov/files/CLPT/documents/2022_ASTR_for_CY2020_FINAL.pdf) warrantless searches of Americans’ communications in 2021 alone, thinks that the answer to its misuse of mass surveillance of Americans is to do more of it, not less.
