![US flag with spying eyes for stars](https://www.eff.org/files/styles/resized_banner/public/banner_library/flag-surveillance-color.jpg?itok=RdvIKri2)

In an unprecedented move, the U.S. Department of Treasury and the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) recently [reached an agreement](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/04/08/us/politics/irs-ice-tax-data-deal.html?unlocked_article_code=1.-E4.4bJn.qa9zpQxCBZPm&smid=nytcore-ios-share&referringSource=articleShare) allowing the IRS to share with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) taxpayer information of certain immigrants. The redacted 15-page [memorandum of understanding](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.dcd.278147/gov.uscourts.dcd.278147.30.1_1.pdf) (MOU) was exposed in a court case, [*Centro de Trabajadores Unidos v. Bessent*](https://www.citizen.org/litigation/centro-de-trabajadores-unidos-v-bessent/), which seeks to prevent the IRS from unauthorized disclosure of taxpayer information for immigration enforcement purposes. Weaponizing government data vital to the functioning and funding of public goods and services by repurposing it for law enforcement and surveillance is an affront to a democratic society. In addition to the human rights abuses this data-sharing agreement empowers, this move threatens to erode trust in public institutions in ways that could bear consequences for decades...
