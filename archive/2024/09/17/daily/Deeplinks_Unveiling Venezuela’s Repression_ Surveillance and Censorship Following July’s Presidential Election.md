*This is part one of a series. Part two on the legacy of Venezuela’s state surveillance is [here](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/09/unveiling-venezuelas-repression-legacy-state-surveillance-and-control).*

As thousands of [Venezuelans took to the streets](https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/aug/13/venezuela-un-human-rights-election-protest) across the country to demand transparency in July’s election results, the ensuing repression [has been described](https://provea.org/actualidad/informe-especial-gobierno-de-maduro-rompe-cifras-historicas-de-represion-en-venezuela/) as the harshest to date, with technology playing a central role in facilitating this crackdown.
