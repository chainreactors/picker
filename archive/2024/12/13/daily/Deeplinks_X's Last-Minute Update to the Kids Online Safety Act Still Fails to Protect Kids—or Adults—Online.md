Late last week, the Senate released yet another version of the Kids Online Safety Act, written, reportedly, with the assistance of X CEO Linda Yaccarino in a flawed attempt to [address the critical free speech](https://www.nationalreview.com/news/time-is-running-out-senator-blackburn-parents-make-final-push-to-pass-kids-online-safety-act/) issues inherent in the bill. This last minute draft remains, at its core, an unconstitutional censorship bill that threatens the online speech and privacy rights of all internet users.

[TELL CONGRESS: VOTE NO ON KOSA](https://act.eff.org/action/tell-congress-kosa-will-censor-the-internet-but-won-t-help-kids "DONATE TODAY ")

no kosa in last minute funding bills

### **Update Fails to Protect Users from Censorship or Platforms from Liability**

The most important update, according to its authors, supposedly minimizes the impact of the bill on free speech. As we’ve said before, KOSA’s “duty of care” section is its biggest problem, as it would force a broad swath of online services to make policy changes based on the content of online speech.
