*This is part two of an ongoing series. Part one on unjust content moderation is [here](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/07/digital-apartheid-gaza-unjust-content-moderation-request-israels-cyber-unit).*

Since the start of the Israeli military response to Hamas’ deadly October 7 attack, U.S.-based companies like Google and Amazon have been [under pressure](https://www.notechforapartheid.com/) to reveal more about the services they provide and the nature of their relationships with the Israeli forces engaging in the military response.

We agree. Without greater transparency, the public cannot tell whether these companies are complying with human rights standards—both those set by the [United Nations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/reference-publications/guiding-principles-business-and-human-rights) and those they have publicly [set for](https://about.google/intl/ALL_us/human-rights/) [themselves](https://sustainability.aboutamazon.com/human-rights/principles).
